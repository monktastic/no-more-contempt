
Bring to mind the "bad people of the world" — the murderers, the rapists, the child abusers; the greedy bankers, hypocritical CEOs, corrupt politicians, etc. What enables them to cause harm without compunction? Something is blocking them from fully perceiving the *life* in their victims — be they other people, animals, or Nature herself.

Now consider how much harm has been caused in the name of *fighting* evil. Didn't Hitler believe that he was doing the right thing? Did any part of him know better? How could you ever be sure that you're not falling into the same trap?

The truth is, no amount of thinking, no matter how careful, can ever be enough. You cannot *calculate* whether you are doing "the right thing."


---

It is not possible to think or calculate your way to goodness. For the same reason, it is impossible for me to convince you of this. It may be possible, however, to give you hints toward remembering it — because something in you already knows.

The deepest part of you wants nothing more than to be of service to the world. The same is true of literally everybody else — but it doesn't feel like that, does it?
