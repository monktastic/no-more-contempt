"## The Trance

Here's the thing about darkness: it has no independent existence.

It's not a substance. It's not a thing. It's just... absence.

When you turn away from your Light—even for a moment, even for what seems like a good reason—something else rushes in to fill the void. A counterfeit. A false north star.

At first, you can tell the difference. The counterfeit feels slightly off. Slightly hollow. You might even notice yourself making excuses: _"Just this once."_ _"They started it."_ _"The world is dark anyway."_

But follow it long enough, and something shifts.

The counterfeit begins to feel real. Natural. True.

Not because it looks anything like your Light—but because you've forgotten what your Light felt like. You've been lured into a trance.

Think of the most hateful person you know. The one who seems genuinely convinced that their cruelty is righteousness. Who cannot even _conceive_ that they might be wrong.

They're not evil in some ontological sense. They're not lacking some goodness-substance that you have.

They're just... asleep. Deep in the trance. So far from their Light that they've completely forgotten it exists. The blazing sun of their deepest nature is still there, has always been there—but they're facing the wrong direction, following a candle, convinced it's the brightest thing in the universe.

And here's what's terrifying: you do this too.

Maybe not as far. Maybe not as obviously. But every time you feel contempt, every time you write someone off, every time you feel that surge of righteous hatred—you're turning away. Following the counterfeit. Forgetting.

The question isn't whether you do this. The question is: how far have you wandered? And do you remember the way back?"
