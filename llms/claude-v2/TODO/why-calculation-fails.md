## The Structural Problem (What You've Already Shown)

### 1. Calculation Happens FROM a Place, Not TO a Place

**The mechanism:**
- When disconnected from Light: thinking serves your desires
- When connected to Light: thinking serves truth
- You can't use thinking to determine which state you're in
- Because thinking itself is different in each state

**From Part II:**
> "Every calculation was in service of the conclusion I'd already reached. Every analysis carefully avoided the parts that would contradict what I wanted. My intellect wasn't finding truth--it was building a fortress around my desires."

### 2. The Limbo State Infects the Calculator Itself

**The problem:**
- You're in limbo about being in limbo
- You use thinking to check if your thinking is contaminated
- But contaminated thinking can't detect its own contamination
- It FEELS clean while being infected

**From Part II:**
> "That's what made it so insidious. My thinking felt clean. It looked clean. But it was just justification wearing the mask of analysis."

**From key-points.md:**
> "We only hear the part of our mind that cleverly calculates how our behavior is somehow a net good."

### 3. Calculation Can't Distinguish Clean from Contaminated

**Both feel rational:**
- Contaminated calculations feel rigorous, logical, justified
- Clean knowing also feels certain
- But they're different IN KIND, not degree
- Calculation can't tell which is which

**From Part II:**
> "The infection went deeper than my actions, deeper than my feelings. It infected my very capacity to know what was right."

### 4. The Cultural Trap: Fetishizing Rationality

**From key-points.md:**
> "This is why our culture fetishizes rationality and intellect above all else: those aspects of mind are fundamentally incapable of detecting the spreading infection until after it has physically manifested, by which point it is too late. We trust our thinking minds to tell us when they're lying to us, and then use more thoughts to prove that thinking is all we have. If there really were an 'inner Light,' we would be able to prove it; and since we can't, there's no point in looking for it."

**The trap:**
1. Parasite convinces us thinking is our only reliable tool
2. We use thinking to verify this claim about thinking
3. Thinking confirms itself (circular reasoning)
4. Anyone suggesting "inner knowing" gets mocked as anti-intellectual
5. Parasite stays hidden in the very tool we use to look for it
